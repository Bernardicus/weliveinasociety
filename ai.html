Local Youth Uses AI to Create Fake Friends
Kyle Kramer - 17 Mar. 2024

Starting yesterday, a well-known unemployed youth, Brendan “kys” Kubin, began to undertake his most recent digital project. Kubin, who has regularly displayed interest in computers and coding, began the process of cloning his friends' voices. Kubin started his quest by collecting vocal samples from his friends. Then, using AI technology via ElevenLabs, each person’s particular vocal intonations are synthesized into a perfect replica. While this is an interesting project for such a young person to undertake, and it certainly highlights Kubin’s affinities for technology, some have expressed concern over the implications of the project. For starters, Mr and Mrs Kubin have expressed their concerns that the ability to mimic time with friends without true interaction may be detrimental to their son’s social life. These worries were espoused by the very friends that Brendan has attempted to clone as well. In an interview, Locke “Puzzle Master” Scogin, who was recently in the paper following an injury, called Kubin’s steps towards robotic companionship “kinda cringe,” saying that Kubin should “try talking to real people.” The concerns extend beyond this, too.

Many are concerned, both generally and in this particular case, about actions that directly contribute to the growing power of AI machinery. ElevenLabs self-identifies as a “voice AI research & deployment company,” making active efforts to improve their services and, thus, AI as a whole. The typical fear is that this development is, either metaphorically or literally, training our future overlords. For this reason, many are inclined to oppose any and all AI applications. In fact, to somewhat conflicting effect, Alex “The Multitasker” Alvarez, whose voice was one of the many cloned by Kubin, expressed his deep concerns as follows: “It's going to take over our brains.” While Alvarez seems to be on the more extreme side of those opposed, his fears are warranted and reflect a general trend of distrust about the technology in general. Even still, more issues remain to be addressed.

Though not as big of an issue in Kubin’s particular case, there are concerns more broadly about the social justice implications of using AI. For example, Kubin has used AI to clone the voice of former President Obama. While he has no plans to use it for any malicious purposes, some have ethical concerns about the prospect of a white person being able to “put words in the mouth” of such a prominent black figure. But for the time being, this concern is unlikely to see an issue manifest in kind.

The community will remain wary and vigilant moving forward, determined not to allow any harm to befall them. Until they feel the need to stage an intervention, they will look on in anticipation of Kubin’s future projects and the further development of this one.
